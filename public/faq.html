What is a National Criminal History Report?

A National Criminal History Report is a point in time check of an individual’s criminal history. It utilises a database that comprises of the records of all Australian Police jurisdictions including: Australian Federal Police

  • National Criminal History Reports can also be referred to as:
  • National Police Check
  • National Police History Check
  • National Criminal History Check
  • National Criminal Record Check

What is the difference between a National Criminal History Report from CrimCheck and the State Police?

They are the same thing, as an accredited agency with the Australian Criminal Intelligence Commission there is no difference in the quality of the results that are released.

How long does it take to process a National Criminal History Report?

84% of checks are returned within 1 day, some will be returned within minutes. Some checks willtake longer to process as there may be people with similar names as known criminals who have a criminal history that need to be verified. There may also be delays in determining results when there are reportable criminal histories.

What is included in a National Criminal History Report certificate?

A National Criminal History Report involves identifying and releasing any Police history information held about an individual, from all Australian States and Territories based on the purpose of the check. The results will indicate either: No Disclosable Court Outcomes (NDCO) - to indicate that there is no Police History Information that is held or that can be released Disclosable Court Outcomes (DCO) - to indicate that Police History Information exists that can be released.

The National Criminal History Report Information may include:

  • Pending matters awaiting court hearing
  • Charges
  • Court convictions – including any penalty or sentence
  • Findings of guilt with no conviction
  • Good behaviour bonds or other court orders

The Police History Information which is disclosed in the results is determined by each Police Agency, based on Spent Conviction Legislation and Information Release Policies. The purpose of the National Police Check and any other relevant legislation also determines what information can be released.

When do I need an Australian Federal Police check instead of a National Criminal History Report?

For Immigration or Visa purposes you will be required to source your National Criminal History Check through the Australian Federal Police, for all other purposes a National Criminal History Report is acceptable throughout Australia

Why is my Police Check taking so long?

Your check can be referred to state police agencies for further investigation because your details are similar or the same as someone who has a reportable criminal history. This can take several weeks to ensure that any criminal history information is not inadvertently released for the wrong person, or that any criminal history information is released in line with the varying state and federal information release acts and policies.

Can I call the Australian Criminal Intelligence Commission to discuss the progress of my National Criminal History Report?

The Australian Criminal Intelligence Commission is not in a position to discuss the progress of any applications, please contact CrimCheck or the organisation that you have lodged your check through.
